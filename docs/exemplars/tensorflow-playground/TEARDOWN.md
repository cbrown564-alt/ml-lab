# TensorFlow Playground

**Concept:** live neural-network training (features, layers, activation, regularization)
**Source:** https://playground.tensorflow.org/
**Our nearest exhibit:** future *neural-network fundamentals* (Phase 1 deep-learning cluster)

## Why it clears the bar

- **One screen, everything live, still calm** (`00-viewport.png`). A transport row (epoch, ▶/step, learning rate, activation, regularization, problem type) spans the top; a data/features rail sits left; the network renders in the centre; a live output heatmap sits right. Enormous information density held together by a disciplined grid and a muted palette — proof that "dense" need not mean "cluttered" (our A5 challenge for advanced exhibits).
- **The weights are the visualization.** Edges between neurons encode magnitude as **thickness** and sign as **colour** (blue/orange diverging). You watch the network *think* — the picture updates every epoch.
- **No prose required.** The whole page is a single live experiment with a transport. Pure "manipulate to understand" (our B1).

## What to steal for the template / exhibits

- **Thickness-encoded, sign-coloured edges** for the neural-network cluster.
- A blue/orange diverging semantic for signed quantities (fits our grammar).
- The "everything live on one screen, governed by a transport" pattern for expert-tier exhibits.

## What NOT to copy

Playground has **near-zero scaffolding** — it's an expert sandbox, not a lesson. Unguided exploration underperforms guided discovery (our B1 research base). Wrap the density in scenarios, prompts, and a failure gallery; never ship the bare sandbox.
