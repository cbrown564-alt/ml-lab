# The Pudding — Film Dialogue

**Concept:** editorial data journalism (gender breakdown of film dialogue)
**Source:** https://pudding.cool/2017/03/film-dialogue/
**Why it's here:** the bar for editorial type, layout, and large-dataset interaction

## Why it clears the bar

- **The chart is the headline.** At `02-scroll-40pct.png` a per-film breakdown of dialogue by gender runs the full width, bars in a two-category colour grammar, with filter chips (All Genres / Action / Drama / Comedy / Family), a search box, and confident editorial typography (the Disney section even carries the brand mark). The data leads; the prose supports.
- **Large-dataset interaction done calmly:** filter + search over thousands of rows without overwhelming the reader. Directly relevant to our A1 measure ("the graph explorer must stay legible and *filterable* at 30+ nodes") — a problem we have deferred and Pudding has solved.
- **Type as a first-class design element:** generous measure, strong hierarchy, an editorial voice. Our shell is clean but typographically timid by comparison.

## What to steal for the template / exhibits

- Editorial typographic confidence (hierarchy, measure, voice).
- Filter/search affordances on large collections — prototype here, then apply to the graph explorer.
- The chart-as-headline instinct.

## What NOT to copy

Pure journalism has **no assessment, no mastery, no graph** — it informs once and ends. We are a lab: keep the retrieval-practice checks and the onward graph movement that turn a read into learning.
