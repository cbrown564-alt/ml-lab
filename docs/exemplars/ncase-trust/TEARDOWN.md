# Nicky Case — The Evolution of Trust

**Concept:** game theory, the iterated prisoner's dilemma (an explorable *game*)
**Source:** https://ncase.me/trust/
**Why it's here:** the model for assessment-as-play and warm, low-ceremony onboarding

## Why it clears the bar

- **Zero-ceremony entry** (`00-viewport.png`): a hand-drawn title, a network-of-faces motif, a single **"PLAY →"** button and a sound toggle. One decision to start — the opposite of a tutorial gauntlet (our A1: first payoff within 60s, no wall).
- **The whole piece is assessment-as-play.** You make choices against opponents, watch consequences accrue, and *derive* the payoff matrix by living it — never a quiz. This is our B5 ideal ("`experiment-task` items keep assessment inside the simulation — assessment as continued play") executed at full length.
- **Consequence-driven learning:** understanding arrives through outcomes you caused, not assertions you read — guided discovery with real stakes.

## Mapped to our criteria

| Criterion | What they do | Where we are (Phase 0) | The gap |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| B5 Assessment | Entire experience is play-as-assessment | We have one `experiment-task` per exhibit | Make the *whole* arc playable where it fits |
| B1 Experiment teaches | Guided discovery with consequences | Scenarios + tasks (good) | Longer consequence chains |
| B6 Delight | Warmth, voice, low-ceremony joy | Disciplined but reserved | More warmth at framing moments |

## What to steal for the template / exhibits

- Assessment-as-play structure — consequence chains over recognition quizzes.
- One-button, warm onboarding for an exhibit's entry.
- Permission to be warm at framing/motivational moments (the persona-effect window in our B4).

## What NOT to copy

The **hand-drawn, whimsical register** clashes with our house voice ("The Economist meets 3Blue1Brown… never cute for its own sake", `PRODUCT.md`). Steal the *structure and warmth*, not the cartoon aesthetic — register mismatch would read as condescension to our adult learner.
